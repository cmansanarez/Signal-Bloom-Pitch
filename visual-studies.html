VISUAL
STUDIES

Building a mythology for emergence.

The images collected here are not finished artworks. They are visual research — concept studies, world-building exercises, prompt experiments — made while developing the symbolic language that now informs the live audiovisual performances.

The most important shift in that process was a change of question. Early on, image generation meant producing isolated pictures: prompt in, artifact out. The work began to cohere only when the question changed. Not what should this image look like, but: what world does Signal Bloom actually inhabit?

The first reference point was Hieronymus Bosch's The Garden of Earthly Delights. Not as something to recreate — what mattered was the method. Bosch built an entire symbolic ecosystem: recurring organisms, impossible architecture, hybrid bodies, strange plants, interconnected narratives. One painting. One world. Endlessly generative.

Signal Bloom adopted that structure. The project would develop its own mythology, its own ecology, its own recurring visual species. Rather than generating unrelated images, every study would belong to a single evolving computational world. The movements of the performance do not illustrate separate scenes — they reveal different states of that world.

A second milestone was NoirmakStyle, a custom Flux LoRA trained on a curated dataset built around the project's own visual language. The earliest successful generations from that model form the core of this archive. They are shown here as they were made: as studies, not illustrations.

Ω

REFLECTION

These studies are not storyboards. The live performance will never recreate these scenes exactly.

They are visual research. Every image in this archive helped define the symbolic vocabulary of Signal Bloom — its species, its architecture, its weather.

What the studies established is not a set of pictures but a computational world capable of continuously generating new ones.

Signal Bloom is not about producing images. It is about designing conditions where worlds can emerge.

Archive continues. Further studies will be appended as the system evolves.
